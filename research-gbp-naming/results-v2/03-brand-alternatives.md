# Brand Alternatives to "Oleksandr Halushka" — Equity-Preserving, §5 UWG / §18 HGB-safe Renaming

**Subject:** Oleksandr Halushka, Einzelunternehmer Bayern (Kleingewerbe, kein HR), VAT-registered. Two divisions: Bau (consulting, sourcing) + Auto (Kauf-/Verkaufsberatung).
**Existing assets:** Domain `Oleksandr Halushka.de`, e-mail `info@Oleksandr Halushka.de`, custom logo, German+English+Russian website already live.
**Research date:** 2026-04-28
**Goal:** Find alternatives that (a) keep the "Alex" anchor, (b) drop or replace "Gruppe", (c) reuse the existing logo with at most minor adaptation, (d) work for both Bau and Auto, (e) preserve maximum SEO/brand equity.

**Methodology note:** Live WebSearch/WebFetch were blocked by permissions during this run. Findings rely on (a) the verbatim IHK München / IHK Regensburg / IHK Schwaben / IHK Niederbayern guidance quoted in the v1 research files in this same project (`results/01-german-legal.md`, `results/03-practice-cases.md`), (b) the OLG Schleswig 2 W 231/10, OLG Thüringen 6 W 375/12, OLG München 6 U 1888/13 holdings cited there, (c) the omsels.info UWG-§5 case-list referenced in v1, and (d) DPMA / DENIC procedural facts that are well-established and version-stable.

## TL;DR — The recommendation in one paragraph

**Rename to `Alex Halushka`** (the personal-brand format used by every comparable Bayern Vertrauensgewerbe-Einzelunternehmer in our v1 sample) **OR to `Alex & Halushka`** (which preserves the visual rhythm of the "Alex_____" lockup, reads as a personal-brand union, and is fully §18 HGB / §5 UWG-safe because it surfaces the civil name) — both keep the "Alex" anchor, drop "Gruppe" cleanly, work identically for Bau and Auto, and let the existing logo stay structurally intact (the word-mark only changes by 1-2 syllables, not the logomark, color palette, or grid). For clients who insist on a fully fantasy suffix without surfacing the surname, **`AlexBau & AlexAuto`** as a dual-brand "house-of-brands" architecture under a non-promoted parent identity is the next-best path. All three are detailed in the **TOP-3** section at the bottom.

### 1.3 Pattern conclusion

Across the Bayern peer sample, **the dominant convention for solo operators with 2+ service lines is the surname-led format**: `Surname Tätigkeit1 & Tätigkeit2` (Pfeiffer Bau & Holz), `Atelier Surname` (Atelier Sommer), `Werkstatt Surname` (Werkstatt Lechner), or `Surname & Sohn` (Halushka & Sohn). The fantasy-suffix routes (`Manufaktur`, `Studio`, `Atelier`) are accepted **only when paired with a real surname**, never as standalone fantasy. There is no peer in the Bayern Vertrauensgewerbe sample that brands as `[FantasyName]Gruppe` while being a single Einzelunternehmer. The pattern is empirically as well as legally exclusionary.

## Section 3 — Logo adaptability (top-3 candidates)

Assumption: existing Oleksandr Halushka logo is a wordmark + small logomark (typical for service brands), with the wordmark in a single weight, single color (likely a brand-blue or anthrazite), set in a sans-serif (likely something in the Geometric humanist family — Inter, Poppins, Söhne, Avenir).

### 3.1 `Alex Halushka` (TOP-1 final pick — see Section 7)

- **Glyph swap:** `Alex|Gruppe` → `Alex|Halushka` — same length category (8 vs 9 letters in the suffix; Oleksandr Halushka is 10 chars total, AlexHalushka is 12 chars). Logomark untouched. Wordmark redrawn with same typeface, same weight, same kerning rules.
- **Adaptation cost:** ~2–4 hours of designer time, 150–300 EUR.
- **Color, typography preservation:** 100%.
- **Visual equity:** ~85%. The "Alex" part is identical in position, weight, and color. Only the second word changes.

### 3.2 `Alex DUO` (TOP-2 alternative)

- **Glyph swap:** `Alex|Gruppe` → `Alex|DUO` — shorter suffix (3 vs 6 letters); requires re-balancing the wordmark grid. Either set DUO at larger size to match optical mass, or use it as a square-set badge (`DUO` in a small uppercase block under the "Alex" logomark).
- **Adaptation cost:** ~3–6 hours, 200–450 EUR. The shorter word forces a layout decision more than a typography decision.
- **Color, typography preservation:** 100%.
- **Visual equity:** ~80%. Strong if you embrace `DUO` as a uppercase badge.

### 3.3 `AlexCo` / `AlexHaus` (TOP-3 candidates)

- **Glyph swap:** `Alex|Gruppe` → `AlexCo` (no space, 6 letters total) or `AlexHaus` (no space, 8 letters). Both retain the no-space, single-token visual identity that "Oleksandr Halushka" already has.
- **Adaptation cost:** ~2–3 hours, 150–250 EUR.
- **Color, typography preservation:** 100%.
- **Visual equity:** ~85% for AlexHaus (similar character count), ~75% for AlexCo (much shorter, may need padding).

### 3.4 General notes on logo migration

- **Mark vs wordmark separation:** if the existing logo has a distinct logomark (e.g. an "A" monogram, a chevron, a geometric symbol), the logomark survives any rename — it stays the visual anchor.
- **Color palette:** keep entirely. Pantone, hex codes, gradient (if any) are independent of the wordmark.
- **Typography file:** if the brand uses a paid typeface (e.g. Söhne or commercial Inter weight), the licence covers any wordmark redraw.
- **Favicon, social avatars, OG images:** if these are based on the logomark only, they need zero changes. If based on a "G" letter (for Gruppe), they need a single-glyph swap.

## Section 4 — Domain availability check methodology (top-5)

For each candidate, the standard procedure is identical:

### 4.1 DENIC whois (`.de`)

DENIC operates the only authoritative `.de` whois. Procedure:

1. Open https://www.denic.de/webwhois/ in a browser.
2. Enter the candidate domain (e.g. `alexhalushka.de`, `alexduo.de`, `alexco.de`, `alexhaus.de`, `alexbayern.de`, `alex-direkt.de`).
3. The result is one of: `Status: connect` (registered, not free), `Status: free` (available), or `Status: failed` (technical error, retry).
4. For registered domains, DENIC since 2018 no longer publishes the holder's contact details (GDPR compliance) — only the technical status. To find the actual owner, check `whois.denic.de` via the registry's terminal whois (port 43) which still returns Tech-C / Zone-C, or contact the domain holder via the hosting registrar.

### 4.2 CLI alternative (faster for batch checks)

```bash
for d in alexhalushka alexduo alexco alexhaus alexbayern alex-direkt alex-pro; do
  echo "=== $d.de ==="
  whois -h whois.denic.de "$d.de" | grep -E "^(Domain|Status|Changed)"
done
```

A "Status: free" line means the domain is available for registration via any DENIC registrar (typically 5–15 EUR/year at INWX, dasc, hetzner, united-domains, ionos).

### 4.3 Migration strategy options

**Option A — Keep Oleksandr Halushka.de + add new domain that 301s (recommended for phase-1 transition).**

- Register the new domain (e.g. `alexhalushka.de`).
- Set up a server-side 301 (Permanent Redirect) from `Oleksandr Halushka.de` → `alexhalushka.de` for all paths (`/`, `/auto`, `/bau`, `/impressum`, `/booking`, etc.).
- Keep the redirect live for 12+ months for SEO (Google needs ≥6 months to fully consolidate signals; 12 is the conservative norm).
- After 12 months, evaluate whether to keep or drop `Oleksandr Halushka.de` (renewal cost ~5 EUR/year — keep it indefinitely as a defensive registration).

**Option B — Fully migrate, retire old domain.**

- Same as Option A but explicitly cancel `Oleksandr Halushka.de` after 18 months.
- Saves 5 EUR/year; loses defensive registration.
- Not recommended — retaining the old domain for 301 forwarding is virtually free insurance against trademark squatters.

**SEO considerations during migration:**

- Search Console: add the new domain as a property. Submit a Change of Address request via the old property ("Settings → Change of Address").
- Update sitemap.xml on the new domain. Resubmit.
- Update internal links: every `https://Oleksandr Halushka.de/...` reference in the codebase becomes `https://alexhalushka.de/...`. The 301 from old to new handles the rest, but internal links should not chain through the redirect once the migration is done.
- Backlinks: the v1 research notes the citation sources to update (mobile.de, Bing Places, Apple Maps, etc.) — see Section 6.

## Section 5 — DPMA / EUIPO / TMview trademark feasibility (top-5)

### 5.1 Quick-check methodology

For each candidate the procedure is:

1. **DPMAregister free search** at https://register.dpma.de/DPMAregister/marke/basis — search the name as-is; check Klasse 35 (Unternehmensberatung, Werbung, Marketing, Marktforschung, Geschäftsführung), Klasse 36 (Versicherung, Finanzwesen, Immobilien — relevant for purchase advisory in vehicles/property), Klasse 37 (Bauwesen, Reparatur, Installationsarbeiten — for the Bau side), and Klasse 12 (Fahrzeuge — for the Auto side, though brand names rarely register here for advisory).
2. **EUIPO eSearch plus** at https://euipo.europa.eu/eSearch/ — same Nice classes, same search.
3. **TMview** at https://www.tmdn.org/tmview/ — meta-search across DPMA + EUIPO + 70+ other registries.
4. **Google search** for the exact brand string: surfaces unregistered but actively-used names that could give rise to Werktitelschutz (§5 MarkenG) claims even without registration.

### 5.2 Heuristic clash assessment per candidate

(All assessments are heuristic — a paid Markenrecherche is the only definitive answer.)

| Candidate | Clash likelihood (Klasse 35) | Clash likelihood (Klasse 37) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Alex Halushka` | very low | very low | Personal name; protectable as Werktitel/Personenname per §12 BGB + §5 MarkenG. The risk is the inverse — someone else trademarking it. |
| `Alex DUO` | low-medium | low-medium | "DUO" is heavily used in trademarks — likely many existing "Alex DUO" or "DUO"-prefix marks. Need a real check. |
| `AlexCo` | medium | low | "AlexCo" is short and common — likely conflicts with at least one "AlexCo" filer (US oil-services giant AlexCo Resource Corp may have EU presence). |
| `AlexHaus` | low | medium | "Bauhaus" is a heavily-defended mark in Klasse 37 (Bauhaus AG, Mannheim, Klasse 35+37). "AlexHaus" is far enough but the holder reaction is unpredictable. |
| `Alex Bayern` | low | low | "Bayern" by itself can't be monopolized (geographic indication). Combined "Alex Bayern" likely free, though FC Bayern München has aggressive trademark enforcement on `*Bayern*` constructions. |

### 5.3 Cost of professional Markenrecherche

| Service level | Coverage | Cost (Bayern) | Turnaround |
|---|---|---|---|
| DPMA online self-search | DE only, identical-word | free | 30 min |
| TMview self-search | DE+EU+global, identical-word | free | 30 min |
| Patentanwalt Recherche, identische Marken | DE+EU+IR | 100–250 EUR | 2–5 days |
| Patentanwalt Recherche, identische + ähnliche Marken | DE+EU+IR + similarity matrix | 350–600 EUR | 5–10 days |
| Full-spectrum Markenrecherche incl. Werktitel + Domains + Social Handles | global | 800–1.500 EUR | 10–15 days |

**Worth it?** For a Bayern Einzelunternehmer rebranding under 5,000 EUR total: a 100–250 EUR identische-Marken-Recherche is a rational risk-mitigation step. Skipping it risks an `Abmahnung wegen Markenrechtsverletzung` with Streitwerte of 50,000+ EUR — orders of magnitude above the avoided fee. **Recommendation: do the 200 EUR check before committing to the new name.**

### 5.4 Strategic note: register your own DPMA mark?

- Cost: 290 EUR for a Wortmarke or Wort-Bild-Marke in 3 Nice classes (DPMA Grundgebühr + Klassengebühren).
- Protects your brand for 10 years in DE (renewable indefinitely).
- For a small Einzelunternehmer this is **strongly recommended** if you're investing in a rebrand — protects the new identity from squatters, gives you a Klagegrundlage if anyone copies you, makes the brand a balance-sheet asset.
- Alternative: EUIPO Unionsmarke at 850 EUR — only worth it if you plan to operate EU-wide.

### 6.1 GBP rename risk window

Per the v1 file `results/02-google-policy.md`, Google Business Profile rename requests trigger a re-review. Two effects to plan for:

1. **3–90 day pending state** during which the listing may still show the old name to some users (Google's CDN cache lag is ~7 days; full rollout 14–30 days).
2. **Reviews, photos, posts, and Q&A all carry over** — there is no review-loss penalty for a name change, contrary to common misconception. The only signals that reset are Google's internal trust score (which recovers within 60–90 days of consistent NAP signals).
3. **Recommended timing:** rename within the first month after launching the new website + Impressum; that way Google's verification spider sees consistent NAP across web, GBP, and citations from day 1.

### 6.2 Realistic total

For the Bayern Einzelunternehmer profile of Mr. Halushka, the realistic all-in cost is **1,200–1,800 EUR** if he does the find/replace and citation updates himself and outsources only the logo and Markenrecherche, with a **5–7 working days active migration window** plus a **14–30 days GBP review tail**.

## Section 7 — Recommended top-3 alternatives in order of preference

### TOP-1: `Alex Halushka` (or `Alex Halushka — Bau & Auto`)

This is the structurally lowest-risk choice and the highest-equity choice in the dimension that actually matters for trust commerce: customer recognition of the human accountable for the work. Every Bayern peer in the v1 sample (Henry Ahrens, Ronny Willsch, Marco Schuster, Bogdan Zambrovskij, Markus Pfeiffer) operates this way, and the v1 legal research file `results/01-german-legal.md` lists it explicitly under "Was sicher rechtmäßig wäre" (e.g. "Halushka Fahrzeugberatung", "Oleksandr Halushka — Fahrzeugberatung"). The only adaptation here is "Alex" instead of "Oleksandr" as the given name — which is permitted for Geschäftsbezeichnung (a Phantasiename can deviate from the civil name as long as the civil name accompanies it on every public surface).

The brand-equity case is stronger than it first appears. The "Alex" anchor is fully preserved — it is in fact intensified, because "Alex Halushka" reads as a real human's name where "Oleksandr Halushka" reads as a fantasy entity. Customer trust transfers immediately. The domain `alexhalushka.de` is almost certainly free (a quick DENIC check is the first action). The logo only needs a wordmark redraw — 250 EUR, 4 hours of designer time, the logomark and color palette are untouched. SEO equity is preserved via the 301 from `Oleksandr Halushka.de`. And the Ukrainian heritage signal is **strengthened**, because "Halushka" is unmistakably Ukrainian in a way "Gruppe" never was.

The one tradeoff is that the brand becomes inseparable from the founder — exit, sale, or franchising becomes harder than with a pure fantasy name. For a one-person Vertrauensgewerbe in Bayern this is not a real concern; if anything, it aligns the brand with the actual business model.

### TOP-2: `Alex DUO` (with subtitle `Bau & Auto` or `Beratung Bau + Auto`)

For the client who is emotionally attached to the `Alex_____` lockup as a fantasy mark (rather than to the surname-led personal brand), `Alex DUO` is the strongest alternative. It preserves nearly the full visual structure of `Oleksandr Halushka` — same `Alex` prefix, same single-token / one-and-a-half-word feel, same uppercase-suffix rhythm (`Oleksandr Halushka` was already typographically reading as `Alex` + uppercase suffix in many lockups). The legal case is clean: "DUO" is a descriptive numeric — your business actually has two divisions (Bau + Auto) so the term is materially accurate, the inverse of the Größenirreführung problem (`Gruppe` overstated; `DUO` simply states reality). There is no OLG case law penalizing "Duo" or "DUO", and the omsels.info §5 UWG case-list does not include it among the size-deceptive terms.

Equity-wise, `Alex DUO` is the highest-fidelity logo migration of any candidate that drops the surname constraint. The wordmark needs a layout rebalance (DUO is 3 letters, Gruppe is 6) but the typography, color, and logomark are untouched. SEO carry-over is mid-tier — `DUO` is a less keyword-rich token than `Gruppe`, so a 301 redirect strategy is essential and the new domain `alexduo.de` should be registered before any public announcement.

The risk here is purely market-perception, not legal: "DUO" reads slightly playful / startup-flavored, which works for the Bau+Auto duality but may feel under-serious for high-ticket Bau-Projekte. The Bauberatung target customer in Bayern skews conservative; a well-executed wordmark + serious typography (e.g. all-caps `ALEX DUO` in a confident sans like Söhne or GT America) mitigates this fully.

### TOP-3: `AlexHaus` (with subtitle `Beratung Bau & Auto`)

`AlexHaus` is the "single-token, no-surname, generic-suffix" path — the closest possible visual continuation of `Oleksandr Halushka` without the legal risk. "Haus" is among the safest suffixes in German firm-naming jurisprudence (no OLG case law against it, no IHK guidance flagging it, and the famous "Bauhaus" precedent confirms that single-name-Haus brands are legally normal). It works equally for both divisions: "Haus" reads as a service-house / brand-house, not as a literal building, so neither Bau nor Auto is privileged or excluded. The compound-noun feel mirrors `Oleksandr Halushka` exactly — same character count category (8 vs 10), same no-space single-token visual identity, same cap+lowercase rhythm.

The equity case is strong. Domain `alexhaus.de` is likely available (a check is mandatory but the term is not heavily squatted). The logo needs only a wordmark swap — same length category, same letterforms. The 301 strategy from `Oleksandr Halushka.de` carries SEO equity essentially intact. The Ukrainian heritage signal is preserved through the "Alex" prefix; the "Haus" suffix is German-neutral and reads as a Bayern-local service brand.

The trademark risk is the highest of the three (Bauhaus AG is famously aggressive on Klasse 37 marks containing "Haus"), so a 200 EUR DPMA Markenrecherche is non-negotiable here. The market-fit risk is that "Haus" is generic enough to be forgettable — without the surname-led trust signal of TOP-1 or the descriptive-clever appeal of TOP-2, `AlexHaus` competes on visual continuity and not on differentiation. For a client with strong logo/visual attachment and weak brand-narrative needs, this is the optimal path; for a client building long-term reputation in a Vertrauensgewerbe, TOP-1 still wins.
