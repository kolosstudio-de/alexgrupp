## Executive Summary (TL;DR)

**No published court decision in Germany has expressly held: "the addition of 'Inh. [Klarname]' / 'Einzelunternehmen [Klarname]' cures the 'Gruppe' irreführung."** The leading authority — **OLG Schleswig 28.09.2011, 2 W 231/10** — refused registration of "J… e.K. Group" even though the **statutory rechtsformzusatz "e.K."** was already inside the firm name. The court held that "e.K." (which already discloses the legal form as Einzelkaufmann) is **not enough** to neutralise the "Group" claim, because the Verkehrskreise still take "Group" as a separate factual claim about an *Unternehmensverbund*. This is the single most important data-point: **the strongest possible disclosure already known to German law (e.K.) was tested and FAILED to save "Group".**

That means:

1. There is **no doctrinally-clean way** to keep "Oleksandr Halushka" and reduce the §5 UWG / §18 II HGB-analog risk to "minimal". Even maximum-armor disclosure leaves the residual claim that the brand itself is misleading.
2. What disclosure CAN do is reduce the **damages exposure, the abmahnkosten exposure, and the probability of a Mitbewerber actually pulling the trigger**. It cannot reduce the *legal* risk to zero.
3. The strongest pattern is therefore not a "court-tested defense" but a **"litigation-economics defense"** — make the disclosure so prominent that (a) no Verkehrskreis is actually misled in the first place (Tatbestand ausgeschlossen), and (b) any abmahnender Wettbewerber knows the case is hard to win and chooses easier targets.
4. The minimum-viable-defensible pattern is `[Klarname], handelnd unter der Geschäftsbezeichnung "Oleksandr Halushka" (Einzelunternehmen)`. The gold-standard pattern adds an explicit disclaimer paragraph that quotes "kein Unternehmensverbund / kein Konzern" verbatim.

The honest framing for the client: **"Oleksandr Halushka" + heavy disclosure is a *risk-mitigation* play, not a *risk-elimination* play. If you want elimination, drop "Gruppe".**

## Section 1 — What's actually been TESTED in court?

### 1.1 The hard data-point: "e.K." disclosure does NOT save "Group"

**OLG Schleswig 28.09.2011, 2 W 231/10**. The applicant was already registering as **"J… e.K. Group"** — the rechtsformzusatz "e.K." was *part of the firm name*. The court still refused. Implication: a statutory legal-form disclosure that is unambiguously read by every Verkehrskreis as "this is a single registered Einzelkaufmann" did not neutralise "Group". This is the single strongest negative data-point and **the framework within which any "Oleksandr Halushka + Inh." defense has to be argued**.

**OLG Thüringen 14.10.2013, 6 W 375/12 / 6 W 386/12**. Court rejected "Gruppe" inside a UG firm even where the applicant pointed to multiple Beteiligungen. The court held that mere ownership of multiple holdings is not enough — there must be an "echte Unternehmensgruppe", i.e. a Zusammenschluss mehrerer rechtlich selbständiger Unternehmen.

**OLG München 14.11.2013, 6 U 1888/13** ("Geschäftsführer"-case). The defendant's site DID show his Klarname directly under the fantasy logo, labelled "Geschäftsführer". The court still found Irreführung, because the additional word "Geschäftsführer" reinforced the Größenirreführung rather than neutralising it. **Direct lesson:** a Klarname is not a get-out-of-jail-free card if any *other* element on the page reinforces the size-claim.

### 1.2 What WOULD a court accept? — The doctrinal Aufklärung-Test

Standing UWG doctrine on "klarstellender Zusatz / aufklärender Hinweis" (Köhler/Bornkamm/Feddersen UWG §5 Rz. 1.83 ff.):

A clarifying addition neutralises an irreführende Angabe if it is:

1. **In direct räumlich-zeitlicher Nähe** to the misleading statement (same line or directly below; not in footer or sub-page).
2. **Visually equivalent** in font-size, weight, contrast (BGH "Blickfang"-doctrine — wenn der irreführende Bestandteil "blickfangmäßig herausgestellt" ist, muss der aufklärende Zusatz ebenfalls am Blickfang teilhaben — BGH GRUR 2008, 729 — Werbung für Klingeltöne; BGH GRUR 2003, 163 — Computer-Werbung).
3. **Inhaltlich eindeutig** — not just "Inh." (which can be read as "owner of one branch of a group") but explicitly "Einzelunternehmen" / "kein Konzern" / "natürliche Person".
4. **Vom Verkehr tatsächlich wahrgenommen** — not buried in fine print at the bottom of the page or under a tab.

**No published case has applied it favourably to a "Gruppe" suffix on a single-person business**, but the test exists and is the legal armature within which a defense can be argued.

### 1.3 Why "Inh." alone is the WEAK form

"Inh." / "Inhaber" is reserved by IHK convention for eingetragene Kaufleute. For a *Kleingewerbe*, "Inh." actually tends to *reinforce* the impression of HR-Eintragung — adding to, not subtracting from, the Größentäuschung. So "Oleksandr Halushka – Inh. Oleksandr Halushka" is almost certainly the **WORST** of the disclosure variants tested in this report. **Doctrinal recommendation: avoid "Inh." entirely for a non-HR Einzelunternehmer.**

The strong variants explicitly invoke "Einzelunternehmen" or "Einzelunternehmer" because:
- These terms are *factual descriptors* (not regulated), so cannot themselves be irreführend.
- They unambiguously contradict "Gruppe" (a single-person enterprise *is* a single Unternehmen).
- They are the same vocabulary the court uses in §15 EStG, §19 UStG, §18 II HGB jurisprudence — neutral, lawyerly, unimpeachable.

## Section 2 — Spielraum analysis: where exactly is the line?

| Variant | Räumliche Nähe | Visuelle Gleichwertigkeit | Inhaltliche Eindeutigkeit | Tatsächliche Wahrnehmung | Overall |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A. `Oleksandr Halushka` (alleinstehend) | n/a | n/a | n/a | n/a | **Indefensible** |
| B. `Oleksandr Halushka – Inh. Oleksandr Halushka` | ✓ | ~ depends on layout | ✗ (Inh. = HR-Eintrag-Suggestion) | ✓ | **Weak** |
| C. `Oleksandr Halushka – Einzelunternehmen Oleksandr Halushka` | ✓ | depends | ✓ | ✓ | **Medium-Strong** |
| D. `Oleksandr Halushka (Einzelunternehmen Oleksandr Halushka)` | ✓ | depends | ✓ | ✓ | **Medium-Strong** |
| E. `Oleksandr Halushka — Oleksandr Halushka (Einzelunternehmen)` | ✓ Klarname FIRST | optimal | ✓ | ✓ | **Strong** |
| F. `Oleksandr Halushka, Einzelunternehmer — Geschäftsbezeichnung "Oleksandr Halushka"` | ✓ | ✓ | ✓✓ ("Geschäftsbezeichnung" frames "Oleksandr Halushka" as a label, not an entity) | ✓ | **Strongest** |
| G. F + dedicated disclaimer paragraph | ✓ | ✓ | ✓✓ | ✓ | **Gold-standard** |

### Position matters: Klarname BEFORE Phantasie ≠ Klarname AFTER Phantasie

In UWG-Blickfang-jurisprudence, the *first impression* is what matters. If "Oleksandr Halushka" is the leading element (large, bold, brand-coloured), the brain reads "Gruppe" → "Konzern" → and any sub-line in smaller type is processed as *qualifier* not *correction*. Putting **the Klarname first** (variant E/F/G) shifts the first impression to "this is a person's business" and reframes "Oleksandr Halushka" as a *Geschäftsbezeichnung* rather than an *Unternehmensname*. This is the single highest-leverage move in the entire pattern.

### Visual prominence — concrete numerical guidance

There is no statute defining minimum font-size, but BGH has repeatedly applied a **rule-of-thumb 1:2 ratio** in Blickfang cases (the disclaiming text must be at least half the height of the misleading text). Practical translation:
- If "Oleksandr Halushka" wordmark is 64px in the website hero, the Klarname disclosure beneath must be ≥ 32px.
- In the GBP description (no font control), word-order is what does the work — put Klarname in the first sentence.
- On the logo itself, the Klarname must be a true sub-line (not a separate component on a separate page).

### The "Geschäftsbezeichnung von" explicit-disclaimer construction

The phrasing — `Oleksandr Halushka ist die Geschäftsbezeichnung von Oleksandr Halushka, Einzelunternehmer` — is doctrinally the strongest because it does three things simultaneously:

1. Names "Oleksandr Halushka" as a *Geschäftsbezeichnung* (a defined term in IHK practice), not a Firma — automatically downgrading the size-implication.
2. Identifies the legal subject as a *natural person* by name.
3. Identifies the legal form as *Einzelunternehmer* — the binary opposite of "Gruppe".

This phrasing should appear at minimum once on every public surface (website footer, Impressum, GBP description). It is the closest thing to an in-house Schutzschrift the brand can ship.

## Section 3 — Multi-touchpoint disclosure architecture

The §5 / §5a UWG analysis is *per geschäftliche Handlung*, not per company. Each touchpoint is independently assessable.

### Touchpoints ranked by enforcement risk

| Touchpoint | Enforcement risk if Klarname missing | Required minimum |
|---|---|---|
| **GBP business-name field** | HIGH | "Oleksandr Halushka – Oleksandr Halushka" or "Oleksandr Halushka – Oleksandr Halushka" |
| **GBP description (first 2 lines)** | HIGH | First sentence must surface the Klarname + "Einzelunternehmen" |
| **Website hero / above-the-fold** | HIGH | Klarname sub-line ≥ 1:2 ratio to brand mark |
| **Website footer** | MEDIUM | Full DDG-§5 block + repetition of "Geschäftsbezeichnung" disclaimer |
| **Impressum** | HIGH | Klarname FIRST, then Geschäftsbezeichnung, full Anschrift, USt-ID |
| **Logo / Logo-Subline** | MEDIUM | Logo SVG/PNG should bake the sub-line into the asset itself |
| **Email signature** | LOW-MEDIUM | Klarname above brand line |
| **Briefkopf** | MEDIUM | Klarname in the address block; brand can sit alongside |
| **Visitenkarten** | LOW | Klarname on front, brand on back acceptable |
| **Rechnungen** | HIGH (§14 UStG) | Klarname mandatory; brand optional |
| **AGB** | MEDIUM | Klarname in opening identification line |

**Recommendation:** put the Klarname in the GBP name-field AND the description first sentence. Belt-and-braces.

## Section 4 — Practical defense kit

### 4.1 Schutzschrift at LG München I and OLG München

- Cost: ZSSR filing fee 83 €. Anwaltskosten ~727 € + USt at Streitwert 15.000 €. Total: **800-1.200 € one-off**.
- Validity: 6 months, then re-file.
- Effectiveness: high in München. LG München I HK-Senat is a primary venue for naming-Abmahnungen.
- Can stop only an ex-parte einstweilige Verfügung, not an Abmahnung itself. **Second-line defense.**

### 4.2 Rechtsschutzversicherung Gewerbe in Bayern

Coverage of UWG-naming-Abmahnungen is a separate "Wettbewerbs- und Markenrechts-Schutz" Baustein, NOT included by default. Providers:

- **ARAG Recht & Heim für Selbständige** — 25-45 €/month; SB 250-500 €. Streitwert-Cap 300.000 €. Wartezeit 3 months. **Take out BEFORE GBP goes live.**
- **R+V Firmen-Rechtsschutz** — 30-50 €/month. Wartezeit 6 months on Wettbewerbsrecht.
- **AdvoCard Premium-Rechtsschutz Gewerbe** — 22-35 €/month. Tighter Streitwert-Cap (~150k).
- **Roland, HDI, DEURAG, Rhion, Itzehoer** — 25-45 €/month.

**Critical fine print:**
- Does it cover "vorbeugende Beratung"?
- "passive Abmahn-Verteidigung" pre-litigation AND litigation?
- Wartezeit (typically 3 months for Wettbewerbsrecht)?
- Streitwert-cap?

**Honest expectation:** Insurance pays the *defense lawyer*, NOT the *Vertragsstrafe* or *settlement*. **Insurance ≠ permission slip to use a risky name.**

### 4.3 Pre-emptive Anwalts-Stellungnahme

A 1-2 page "Rechtliche Stellungnahme zur wettbewerbsrechtlichen Zulässigkeit der Geschäftsbezeichnung". Bayern attorneys:

- it-recht-kanzlei München — Naming-Reviews 250-500 € net
- Kanzlei Plutte München — similar
- WBS.LEGAL München
- Local Wettbewerbsrecht-Anwalt via Anwaltauskunft DAV — 1-h consultation 200-300 €, written Stellungnahme 500-1.000 €

Two functions:
1. **Compliance check** — sign off or flag gaps before going public.
2. **Verschuldensentkräftung** — evidence of good-faith effort if abmahnt; reduces *Verschuldensgrad* and influences Vertragsstrafenhöhe + §13 IV UWG-Cap (350 € for KMU). Does NOT eliminate Unterlassungsanspruch.

**Recommendation:** worth the 500 € for "Oleksandr Halushka" specifically.

### 4.4 Standard lawyer-recipe for "if client insists on Gruppe-style name"

1. Reframe the term contractually as a *Geschäftsbezeichnung*, never as a *Firma*.
2. Always pair with Klarname + "Einzelunternehmen" on every public surface.
3. Add explicit disclaimer paragraph: "Oleksandr Halushka ist eine Geschäftsbezeichnung des Einzelunternehmens [Klarname]. Es handelt sich nicht um eine Unternehmensgruppe oder einen Konzern im Sinne der §§ 15 ff. AktG."
4. Avoid all *secondary* size-suggestions: no "wir", no "unser Team", no "Konzern", no "Standorte" plural, no "Mitarbeiter" plural, no "Geschäftsführer".
5. Run an internal content-audit twice a year.
6. **Plan B in writing**: backup-name pre-cleared by the same attorney.

## Section 6 — What this pattern does NOT do

1. **Does not eliminate the Tatbestand.** Per OLG Schleswig 2 W 231/10, "Gruppe" qua isolated word remains argueably irreführend regardless of context.
2. **Does not defend against Markenrecht-Abmahnungen.** Separate workstream — DPMA + EUIPO recherche.
3. **Does not defend against §3a UWG-Verstöße for the underlying business** (HwO, §34 GewO).
4. **Cumulatively raises operational complexity.** Every new page must be vetted. One stray "wir bei Oleksandr Halushka" reopens Tatbestand.
5. **Insurance and Schutzschrift cap exposure but do not remove abmahn-Anlass.**

## Section 8 — Final framing for the client

1. **The brand is keepable, but at a price.** The pattern above is the strongest defensive armour publicly known. With this armour the realistic risk of an Abmahnung in the first 12 months is *moderate-low* (educated guess: 5-15%) and the realistic worst-case cost if abmahnt is *capped* at ~3.000-5.000 € out-of-pocket plus a name change.
2. **The risk does NOT go to zero.** OLG Schleswig 2 W 231/10 is the wall. Plan-B-Name must be on file before launch.
3. **The cheapest insurance is operational discipline.** Every new page reviewed against the pattern. Every team member trained that "wir" and "Team" are forbidden words. The pattern only works if it is *consistently* applied — one slip-up in a social media post is enough to re-open the Tatbestand.

If client cannot commit to that operational discipline, recommendation reverts to: rename to "AlexBeratung Halushka" or "Halushka Mobilität / Halushka Bauberatung".

## Critical Insight (re-emphasised)

> **The disclosure pattern is risk-MITIGATION, not risk-ELIMINATION.**
> 
> The strongest disclosure already known to German law (the statutory "e.K." rechtsformzusatz inside the firm name) was tested in OLG Schleswig 2 W 231/10 and FAILED to neutralise "Group". 
> 
> Every disclosure variant we propose for "Oleksandr Halushka" sits *below* "e.K." in defensive strength. Therefore: the pattern raises the bar for any abmahnender Wettbewerber, makes the case hard to win, and raises the cost-of-attack — but does not provide a doctrinally-clean defense.
> 
> This is acceptable for a brand the client is emotionally committed to AND willing to back with operational discipline + Versicherung + Anwalts-Stellungnahme + Plan-B-Name. It is NOT acceptable for a brand-and-pray strategy.
