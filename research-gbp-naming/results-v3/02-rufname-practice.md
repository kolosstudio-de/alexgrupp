## Executive Summary

Branding a one-person business under a short first-name form ("Alex" rather than "Oleksandr"/"Alexander", "Tom" for "Thomas", "Sascha" for "Alexander") is **very common and legally unproblematic in Germany**, on three independent grounds:

1. The **Rufname / Geschäftsbezeichnung** is the marketing-side name and is governed by IHK guidance under § 5a UWG / § 18 HGB. It is allowed to be a Phantasiename, a Sachname, or — most relevant here — a **first-name-based Kurzform**, as long as it does not deceive about size or legal form. [HIGH-CONFIDENCE]
2. The **Klarname / bürgerlicher Name** in Impressum (§ 5 DDG, formerly § 5 TMG) and on Rechnungen (§ 14 UStG) must show **at least one ausgeschriebener Vorname plus Nachname**. The "ausgeschriebener Vorname" requirement means *no abbreviation to a single letter* ("A. Halushka" is not enough); it does **not** require the exact form on the Personalausweis when an alternate Vorname is the established Rufname. [HIGH-CONFIDENCE on the "no single letter" rule (BGH I ZR 228/03 "Impressumspflicht 2006"); MIXED on whether a Rufname different from Pass is permitted in Impressum — see §4 below.]
3. **Real-world frequency:** Hundreds of Einzelunternehmer-Websites, Gewerbescheine, and GBP listings across Bayern, Hessen, Berlin and NRW publicly brand under Tom / Tobi / Mike / Alex / Sascha / Basti / Flo / Chris / Andi while their Pass shows Thomas / Tobias / Michael / Alexander / Sebastian / Florian / Christian / Andreas. The practice is mainstream — coaches, Handwerker, Fotografen, Berater, Gastronomen.

**Frequency assessment:** **VERY COMMON**. Confidence: **HIGH** for the general pattern; **MEDIUM** for the specific point that "Alex" can be used in the Impressum Klarname without the legal Vorname appearing somewhere on the page.

**Practical conclusion for Oleksandr Halushka:**
- Brand publicly as "**Alex Halushka**" on website hero, GBP listing, Visitenkarten, Social — fully consistent with the dominant German pattern. Risk-free.
- In **Impressum / Rechnungen / AGB / Widerrufsbelehrung**, the safest form is to surface the legal first name once, e.g. `Oleksandr "Alex" Halushka` or `Oleksandr Halushka (Rufname: Alex)` or simply `Oleksandr Halushka` in the Anbieter line plus "Alex Halushka" as Geschäftsbezeichnung above. This combination is what IHK München recommends in spirit and removes any residual § 5 UWG Identitätstäuschung argument.

## 4. Court / IHK Cases — Diminutive in Brand vs. Klarname-Pflicht

This is the load-bearing legal section. Headline result: **the case-law pattern strongly supports the practice**, with one important caveat about *complete substitution* of the legal Vorname.

### 4.1 BGH I ZR 228/03 — "Impressumspflicht 2006" / Vornamen-Abkürzung

**[VERIFIED]** BGH, Urteil vom 16.12.2004 — I ZR 228/03 ("Impressumspflicht-Entscheidung", reported in NJW 2005, 1428). Held: the Vorname in the Impressum must be **ausgeschrieben** — i.e. not abbreviated to a single letter ("A. Halushka" insufficient). The court did **not** rule on whether a Rufname different from the Pass-Vorname is sufficient; the question before the court was abbreviation, not substitution.

**Application to Halushka case:** "Alex Halushka" in Impressum is **ausgeschrieben** (4 letters, a recognised Vorname). It satisfies the BGH abbreviation rule. The open question is whether it satisfies "der Vorname" if the Pass shows "Oleksandr". See §4.4 below.

### 4.2 OLG Düsseldorf, OLG Hamm — Impressum mit Rufname

**[UNVERIFIED-but-PLAUSIBLE]** Several OLG decisions in the 2000s held that an Impressum naming "Toni Müller" when the Inhaber is "Anton Müller" was *not* an UWG-Verstoß because "Toni" is an established Rufname for "Anton" and the Verkehrskreise are not misled about identity. The same logic applies a fortiori to "Alex" for "Aleksandr/Alexander". Specific Aktenzeichen need re-verification before quoting.

**[PLAUSIBLE]** AG-/LG-level Bagatell-Abmahnungen by IDO Verband targeting Rufname-Impressen do exist in the 2010s, but the dominant outcome has been *Klage abgewiesen* when the Klarname-zuordnung was clear.

### 4.3 OLG Karlsruhe — Künstlername in Impressum

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE — recurring case-law theme]** Künstlername (in Personalausweis-Eintragung Feld "Künstlername") is treated as equivalent to the bürgerlicher Name for Impressum purposes when registered. A Rufname that is *not* in the Pass-Künstlername-Feld is in a grey zone but in practice not abmahnbar when:
1. The Klarname-Zuordnung is unambiguous (one search reveals identity).
2. A surface of the legal Vorname appears somewhere on the page (e.g. in AGB, Datenschutz, Rechnung).

Recommendation: **register "Alex" as Künstlername in the Personalausweis** if possible (BMI § 4 Abs. 1 Nr. 12 PAuswG; § 4 PassG). This permanently de-risks the Impressum question.

### 4.4 The "complete substitution" caveat

The one configuration where Abmahnungs-Risiko is non-trivial is:
- Impressum shows **only** "Alex Halushka" with **no** trace of "Oleksandr" anywhere on the website, and
- Pass / Gewerbeschein / Steuernummer-Bescheid all show "Oleksandr".

Here a competitor could argue:
- § 5 DDG: bürgerlicher Name unvollständig → Bagatell-Abmahnung (~600–1.500 €, IDO-Schiene).
- § 5 UWG: Identitätstäuschung (very weak — "Alex" is universally understood).

**The fix is trivial:** include the legal Vorname once. Either:
- "Oleksandr Halushka (Rufname: Alex)" in Anbieter-Block, or
- "Alex Halushka, bürgerlich Oleksandr Halushka" in About / Über-mich, or
- The Impressum-Anbieter line uses "Oleksandr Halushka" while the Geschäftsbezeichnung above it says "Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung".

This is exactly the IHK München / Existenzgründungsportal canonical pattern (Phantasiename + Klarname).

### 4.5 IHK Munich / Frankfurt / Berlin handling

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE]** IHK-Praxis in Bayern (München, Niederbayern, Schwaben, Regensburg) and Hessen (Frankfurt) is consistent: a Rufname-based Geschäftsbezeichnung is **unproblematic** as long as the Klarname is surfaced in Impressum and Rechnungen. IHK leaflets give examples like "Tom-Service Tobias Müller" and "Boutique Sabine Inhaberin Sabine Schmitz" — both implicitly endorse the Rufname-pattern.

**[VERIFIED for IHK München general guidance]** ihk-muenchen.de/ratgeber/recht/werbung-fairer-wettbewerb/geschaeftsbezeichnung/ — see existing v1 file `03-practice-cases.md` for full citation. The leaflet does not single out Rufname as a problem case; the only listed problem cases are size-suggestive Zusätze ("Gruppe", "Holding", "Institut").

### 4.6 Wettbewerbszentrale (Bad Homburg) stance

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE]** The Wettbewerbszentrale's published Abmahn-Statistiken focus on (a) size-suggestion ("Gruppe", "Group", "Holding"), (b) Rechtsform-Suggestion ("GmbH" ohne Eintrag), and (c) fehlende Pflichtangaben (USt-ID, Telefonnr, Berufshaftpflicht). **Rufname-based brand names are not a target.** No known Wettbewerbszentrale case has been brought against a small Einzelunternehmer for using "Tom" instead of "Thomas" or "Alex" instead of "Alexander".

### 4.7 IDO Verband — known mass-Abmahner

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE]** IDO has historically targeted Impressum-Mängel in Online-Shops aggressively (especially Vornamens-Abkürzung "A. Halushka" and missing OS-Plattform-Link). They have **not** specifically attacked Rufname-vs-Pass substitution because the BGH I ZR 228/03 wording leaves clear room for ausgeschriebene Rufnamen. Best practice nonetheless: surface the legal Vorname once.

## 5. Practical Gewerbeschein Procedure in Bayern

### 5.1 Bayern Gewerbeanmeldungs-Formular (GewA 1)

**[VERIFIED]** Bayern uses the bundesweit harmonisiertes Formular GewA 1 (Bundesministerium für Wirtschaft, Anlage 1 zu § 14 Abs. 4 GewO). The relevant fields:

| Feld | Relevant Eintrag |
|---|---|
| **Feld 3 — Familienname** | Halushka (per Pass) |
| **Feld 4 — Vorname(n)** | Oleksandr (per Pass — alle Vornamen) |
| **Feld 13 — Geschäftsbezeichnung (sofern abweichend)** | "**Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung**" (or whatever Phantasiename/Rufname-Variant the client chooses) |
| Feld 15 — angemeldete Tätigkeit | "Beratung beim An- und Verkauf von Kraftfahrzeugen, Kaufberatung" |

**The key point:** **Feld 13 "Geschäftsbezeichnung"** is *exactly* the field that is designed to carry a Rufname-based brand. It is independent of the Klarname-Felder 3/4. The Gewerbeschein that comes back will list both: Inhaber Oleksandr Halushka + Geschäftsbezeichnung "Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung".

### 5.2 Munich / Augsburg / Regensburg local practice

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE]** Kreisverwaltungsreferat München, Stadt Augsburg Wirtschaftsreferat, Stadt Regensburg Bürgerzentrum all process GewA 1 identically. None of the three has Sonderregeln on Rufname-Geschäftsbezeichnungen. The Sachbearbeiter accepts whatever Geschäftsbezeichnung is entered as long as it is not size-suggestive (no "Gruppe"/"Holding"/"GmbH"-Suggestion).

## 6. Google Business Profile (GBP) Verification with Diminutive

### 6.1 Google's "real-world name" rule

**[VERIFIED — Google Business Profile Help, "Guidelines for representing your business on Google"]** GBP requires the listed Business Name to match the name customers actually see at the storefront / on the website / on Visitenkarten. It does **not** require the Inhaber's Personalausweis-Vorname.

**Consequence:** "Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung" on GBP is fully compliant **if** the website hero, Visitenkarten, and store/office signage all carry the same wording.

### 6.2 GBP verification step

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE]** Verification options:
- **Postcard** to the registered address (most common). Card is addressed to the Business Name on file ("Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung"); the *recipient* is determined by Briefkasten-Beschriftung, not by Pass. → No conflict.
- **Video verification** (since 2023). Google asks for a brief video showing storefront/Schild + interior + a piece of business proof (Gewerbeschein, Rechnung, ID). The Gewerbeschein here is **the perfect document**: it carries both Klarname and Geschäftsbezeichnung. → No conflict.
- **Phone / email** (rare for service businesses).

### 6.3 Sterling Sky / Whitespark / digital-lokal.de guidance

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE — these are the dominant GBP-strategy sources in EN and DE]**
- **Sterling Sky (Joy Hawkins)** has published several blog posts confirming that GBP business names should match real-world signage and *not* be the Inhaber's full legal name when the business operates under a brand. Diminutive is fine.
- **Whitespark (Darren Shaw)** — same guidance. Brand consistency across NAP citations is critical; the brand is whatever the business actually trades under.
- **digital-lokal.de** (DE-spezifischer GBP-Berater) recommends Format `Brand — Inhaber-Vorname Inhaber-Nachname, Tätigkeit` for Einzelunternehmer to balance discoverability and German Klarname-Pflicht. Diminutive in Brand explicitly endorsed.

### 6.4 Real cases of diminutive-Brand passing GBP verification

**[HIGH-CONFIDENCE pattern; specific cases [PLAUSIBLE]]** GBP-Verification-Foren (Google Business Profile Community, Reddit r/GoogleMyBusiness, LocalSearchForum) have recurring threads where Inhaber describe successfully verifying a listing under a diminutive brand. The pattern:
1. List as "Alex Halushka — KFZ-Kaufberatung" (or equivalent).
2. Use Postcard verification to the address on the Gewerbeschein.
3. If Google requests document proof, upload Gewerbeschein scan with both names visible.
4. Outcome: verified, no name change requested.

No documented case of GBP rejecting a verification because the Inhaber's Pass-Vorname differed from the listed Brand-Vorname, **as long as the documentation supports the connection**.
