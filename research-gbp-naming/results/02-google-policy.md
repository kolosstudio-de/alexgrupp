# Google Business Profile Naming Policy - Research for Oleksandr Halushka (Einzelunternehmer)

**Research date:** 2026-04-28
**Subject:** Oleksandr Halushka, German sole proprietor (Einzelunternehmer), Geschäftsbezeichnung "Oleksandr Halushka", domain Oleksandr Halushka.de, vehicle purchase/sale advisory.
**Question:** What name should appear in the GBP "Business name" field?

---

## TL;DR (Executive Summary)

1. Google's official rule (support.google.com/business/answer/3038177) requires the **real-world business name as it is consistently used on storefront, website, stationery, and other branding** - and explicitly forbids "unnecessary information" including marketing taglines, locations, services, or legal-form suffixes.
2. There is **no separate "legal name" field** in standard German GBP - just one public "Business name" field. The "Brand profile" feature (support.google.com/business/answer/16394780, 2024-2025) is for online-only sellers and does not apply here.
3. For a German Einzelunternehmer NOT in the Handelsregister, the **Geschäftsbezeichnung ("Oleksandr Halushka") is legally a supplement to the civil name** and is NOT itself a Firma. The owner's Vor- und Nachname must appear in the Impressum and on invoices/letterhead. However, the **GBP name field should reflect what is on the storefront/website** - which for this business is "Oleksandr Halushka".
4. **Recommended GBP name: `Oleksandr Halushka`** (clean, no suffix, no keywords). The owner's name "Oleksandr Halushka" belongs in the Impressum and on the verification document (Gewerbeschein), not in the GBP name field.
5. **Do NOT add** "Auto Beratung", "Bayern", "KFZ", or any descriptor - this is the #1 suspension trigger documented by Sterling Sky (28% of all suspensions in 2024 were name-related).

## 1. Google's Official Stated Rule

### 1.1 The canonical guideline page

**URL:** https://support.google.com/business/answer/3038177?hl=en
**German version:** https://support.google.com/business/answer/3038177?hl=de
**Title:** "Guidelines for representing your business on Google" / "Richtlinien für die Präsentation Ihres Unternehmens auf Google"

### 1.2 Exact wording (English)

> "Your business name should reflect your business's real-world name, as used consistently on your storefront, website, stationery, and as known to customers."
>
> "Including unnecessary information in your business name isn't permitted, and could result in the suspension of your Business Profile."

### 1.3 What is explicitly NOT allowed in the name field

Per support.google.com/business/answer/3038177 and corroborated by multiple secondary sources (Sterling Sky, BlueCorona, PinMeTo, Birdeye 2026 guidelines):

- **Marketing taglines** ("Best bagels in town for $5!", "Everything on sale -50%")
- **Promotional language** ("Buy", "Sale at")
- **Phone numbers**
- **Sale announcements**
- **Service area / city / region** (unless it is genuinely part of the legal name on the storefront)
- **Service descriptors** ("Plumber", "Auto Beratung") unless part of registered name
- **Legal form suffixes added cosmetically** ("LLC", "Inc.", "Co.", "GmbH" - "frowned upon" in Google's wording, though tolerated when it is genuinely part of the registered Firma)
- **Special characters** outside normal punctuation
- **Combining multiple brands** at one location (e.g. "KFC / Taco Bell")

### 1.4 Country-specific rules for Germany

There are **no Germany-specific name rules** in the Google support documentation. The German version of /answer/3038177 is a 1:1 translation of the English version. The same "real-world name only, no unnecessary information" rule applies.

The German page (/answer/3038177?hl=de) phrases it as:

> "Der Name des Unternehmens sollte so eingegeben werden, wie er offline erscheint, zum Beispiel auf Schildern, Briefköpfen und anderen Marketingmaterialien."
>
> "Es ist nicht erlaubt, dem Namen eines Unternehmens unnötige Informationen hinzuzufügen. Bei Nichtbeachtung kann das entsprechende Unternehmensprofil gesperrt werden."

### 1.5 Keyword stuffing - explicit verdict

**Yes, "Oleksandr Halushka Auto Beratung Bayern" is forbidden** and a documented suspension trigger. Search Engine Land, Sterling Sky and Google's own /answer/3038177 list "Best Plumber in Dallas" / "Joe's Plumbing - Best Plumbers in Chicago" / "Best Dentist Toronto Emergency 24/7" as the canonical examples of the violation pattern.

## 3. Verification Process & Document/Name Match

### 3.1 What documents Google accepts (2024-2026)

For Germany, Google's verification flow accepts (per /answer/14271705 and reputationarm.com guidance):

- **Gewerbeschein / Gewerbeanmeldung** (most relevant for an Einzelunternehmer)
- Utility bill at the business address
- Rental agreement
- Bank statement showing business name and address
- Registered letter/Postkarten code (still default for many German listings)
- Video verification (recording storefront, signage, business documents on camera)

### 3.2 Does the name need to match exactly?

**Not exactly, but the connection must be visible.** Per multiple verification guides (Local Falcon, Digital Harvest 2025, gmbapi.com 2025):

> "You can show business documents like a business permit, invoice, or utility bill that matches the name on your Business Profile."
>
> "Show official business documents that link your name, the business name and address."

The keyword is **"link"** - Google needs to see one document that ties:
1. The owner (Oleksandr Halushka)
2. The business name (Oleksandr Halushka)
3. The business address

### 3.3 Specific case: Gewerbeschein says "Oleksandr Halushka", GBP says "Oleksandr Halushka"

This is the exact Oleksandr Halushka scenario. Three observations from research:

**(a) German law is on the client's side here.** Per IHK München, IHK Regensburg, existenzgruendungsportal.de, and frame-for-business.de:

> "Sole proprietorships not entered in the commercial register are allowed to give their business a [Geschäftsbezeichnung]. This serves to distinguish and market the business. The Geschäftsbezeichnung is a supplement to the civil name; it is not a Firma in the legal sense."

The Gewerbeschein typically has a field "Geschäftliche Bezeichnung" or similar. **If "Oleksandr Halushka" is written there alongside "Oleksandr Halushka", the document already links the two names and Google verification works.**

**(b) If the Gewerbeschein lists ONLY "Oleksandr Halushka":** the client should:
- Re-issue the Gewerbeschein with "Oleksandr Halushka" as Geschäftsbezeichnung at the local Gewerbeamt (typically EUR 20-60, takes 1-2 weeks), OR
- Submit additional documents that link the names: an invoice or letterhead with both "Oleksandr Halushka - Oleksandr Halushka", a bank account statement for "Oleksandr Halushka, Oleksandr Halushka", or website/Impressum showing both names together.

**(c) Tolerance:** Google's verification reviewers (per support.google.com community threads) DO accept Geschäftsbezeichnung when it is documented somewhere - Impressum, invoice, signage, business letterhead. The link does not have to be on a single government document.

### 3.4 Recent (2024-2026) experience reports

- Sterling Sky's 2025 video verification guide notes "even small mismatches like a PO box on a utility bill when your physical location is different can stop your verification."
- Multiple Reddit/localsearchforum threads from 2024-2025 confirm: Google reviewers will accept a brand name when it is consistently shown on website + signage + at least one document, even if it is not the registered legal name.
- April 2025: Google added "double-check video" feature in the verification flow (Sterling Sky video verification guide).

## 4. GBP Fields - Legal vs Public Name

### 4.1 Standard German GBP interface (as of 2026)

There is **only one "Business name" field** ("Unternehmensname") in the German GBP. It must hold the public-facing real-world name.

There is **no separate "Legal entity name" field** in the German interface. (The UK/US GBP also has only one name field; Sterling Sky and BlueCorona confirm this in 2025 articles.)

### 4.2 The "Brand profile" feature (introduced 2024-2025)

Source: https://support.google.com/business/answer/16394780

Google introduced a separate **Brand profile** for online-only sellers. Key facts:

- Brand profile = for **online-only / e-commerce** businesses without a physical storefront.
- Business profile = for businesses with face-to-face customer interaction at a location.
- Both can coexist (e.g. a shop with a webshop).
- The Brand profile shows "Brand profile" as a label under the name; Business profile shows the address.

**This does NOT solve the legal-vs-brand-name question.** The Brand profile is a different product (for online sellers), not a "legal name" extra field. Oleksandr Halushka is a local advisory service, so it uses the Business profile, not Brand profile.

### 4.3 Can you list a public-facing brand and a different legal entity?

**No.** Per support.google.com/business community threads (110747135, 254344213, 382466762) and localsearchforum.com thread 60261:

> "Listing both a legal name and a DBA normally isn't permitted - it's one or the other, not both."

**The decision is binary:** put one name in the Business Name field. The other name appears only on backup verification documents.

### 4.4 What about the Impressum / "About" section?

The GBP profile has free-text fields ("From the business" / "Unternehmensbeschreibung", website, attribution) where the legal name and Impressum can be referenced. This is the place to put "Oleksandr Halushka" - NOT the Business name field.

**Use `Oleksandr Halushka`.** No suffix, no descriptors, no keywords.
